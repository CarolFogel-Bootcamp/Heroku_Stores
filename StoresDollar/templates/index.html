Background

Food Deserts

Low income areas which lack easy access to grocery stores are referred to as 'Food Deserts'. The USDA defines food deserts as parts of the country that lack healthy food, such as fresh fruit and vegetables. These areas are typically impoverished and have a lack of grocery stores, farmers' markets and healthy food providers. Approximately 23.5 million Americans live in food deserts.

Dollar Stores

The Institute for Local Self-Reliance's (ILSR) released a report in 2018 discussing how dollar stores are targeting rural and and low-income neighborhoods that satisy the characteristics of food deserts. This raises concerns because although dollar stores typically sell milk and eggs, they do not sell fresh fruits or vegetables, or other nutrionally-dense food products.
